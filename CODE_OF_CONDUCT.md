# TernaryPhysics Code of Conduct

TernaryPhysics is an open community that builds safety-critical software for the Linux kernel and its userspace companions. Every participant—maintainers, contributors, reviewers, and community members—shares responsibility for making the project a respectful, collaborative, and harassment-free place to learn and build.

## Our Standards

We expect everyone interacting in GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, events, and private channels to:

- Demonstrate empathy, patience, and respect toward other people and their work.
- Assume positive intent and critique ideas, not individuals.
- Provide constructive, actionable feedback backed by data or references when possible.
- Celebrate diverse viewpoints and backgrounds. Invite expertise from security, networking, ML, and kernel communities.
- Share credit for successes and take responsibility for mistakes.

Unacceptable behavior includes, but is not limited to:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks based on identity, experience, or differing opinions.
- Threats, intimidation, or encouragement of self-harm.
- Unwelcome sexual language or imagery.
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or sustained derailing of conversations.
- Publishing others’ private information without explicit permission.
- Conduct that violates applicable laws or jeopardizes the safety of community members.

## Reporting and Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. Capture relevant context (links, screenshots, dates).
2. Report privately to the maintainers through the contact options listed in `SECURITY.md`, or by opening a confidential GitHub Security Advisory addressed to the `KernelPhysics` maintainers.
3. If the report concerns an acting maintainer, please request involvement from another maintainer or a trusted community representative.

Maintainers must review reports promptly, maintain confidentiality where possible, and communicate next steps to all affected parties. Enforcement actions may include warnings, temporary or permanent bans from community spaces, or escalation to hosting providers or legal authorities when required.

## Scope

This Code applies whenever someone officially represents the project (conferences, livestreams, blogs) and within all online project spaces (GitHub, chat, mailing lists). Expect the same professionalism whether you are reviewing a BPF patch, updating documentation, or coordinating a release.

## Continuous Improvement

Kernel, AI, and security communities evolve quickly; so will our norms. Suggestions for improving this Code are welcome via pull requests or discussions. Updates will be announced in the CHANGELOG or release notes when material changes occur.
